Concrete Pickleball Court Specs
Concrete Prep Specs for GCs
We surface over your concrete slab. Here's exactly what the slab needs to look like before we mobilize — no guesswork for your concrete sub.

Slab Requirements

  • Minimum 4" thick, 3,000 PSI concrete
  • Steel-trowel or medium broom finish
  • 1% slope in one plane for drainage
  • No birdbaths or standing water areas
  • Expansion joints at court perimeter only
  • 28-day minimum cure time before surfacing

Court Dimensions

  • Playing area: 20' x 44' per court
  • With run-outs: 30' x 60' minimum
  • Recommended: 34' x 64' per court
  • Multi-court spacing: 10' between courts
  • Net height: 36" at sidelines, 34" at center
  • 4 pickleball courts fit one tennis court slab

Frequently Asked Questions
Pickleball Court Questions GCs Ask Us

What are the standard pickleball court dimensions?

A standard pickleball court is 20 feet wide by 44 feet long. With recommended run-out areas, plan for 30' x 60' minimum per court. For multi-court amenity center layouts, we recommend 34' x 64' per court to allow adequate player spacing between adjacent courts.

How thick is the ATS Acrytech pickleball surface?

The full Acrytech system is typically 4-6 coats applied in layers — resurfacer, filler/leveling coats, color coats, and line paint. Total build-up is approximately 1/16" to 1/8". It bonds directly to the concrete slab, so there's no measurable height change for adjacent trades to worry about.

Can you put pickleball lines on an existing tennis court?

Yes, and we do it regularly. Up to 4 pickleball courts fit on a single tennis court. We use contrasting line colors so both sports are clearly marked without visual confusion. This is a common amenity center conversion — maximizes court usage for minimal additional cost.

How long does pickleball court surfacing take?

A typical 2-4 court amenity center installation takes 5-8 business days, weather depending. We provide an exact timeline in our bid so you can schedule your other trades around us. No delays, no carrying costs from a surfacing sub that can't hit their dates.

What warranty do I get on a pickleball court surface?

Two layers of coverage: ATS Sports Acrytech's manufacturer warranty on materials, plus our 2-year workmanship warranty on the install. Both sets of documentation go straight into your project closeout package. No gap in warranty coverage between installer and manufacturer.

Do you handle pickleball court resurfacing?

Yes. We resurface and recoat existing pickleball courts with ATS Acrytech — crack repair, leveling, new color coats, and fresh line striping. Resurfacing extends court life significantly and costs a fraction of a full rebuild. Good option if the owner's existing courts are fading or peeling.

What concrete spec does my concrete sub need to hit?

Minimum 4" thick, 3,000 PSI, steel-trowel or broom finish, 1% slope in one plane, no birdbaths, 28-day cure. We include a full concrete prep spec sheet with every bid that you can hand directly to your concrete sub — zero back-and-forth.

Can you do multi-sport courts with pickleball?

Yes. We regularly stripe pickleball lines alongside tennis, basketball, and volleyball on shared court surfaces. Each sport gets a distinct line color for clarity. We handle the layout geometry so all game lines are to regulation spec — no change orders from incorrect markings.
