Our Resurfacing Process

How We Resurface Courts for GCs

A predictable, documented process so you know exactly what to schedule and when. No surprises, no change orders.
1

Site Assessment

We inspect the existing surface — cracks, delamination, drainage issues, bird baths. You get a written condition report and a scope recommendation within 48 hours.

2

Surface Prep

Pressure washing, crack filling, and leveling. We address structural deficiencies in the existing coating before any new material goes down. No shortcuts.

3

Acrytech Application

ATS Sports Acrytech acrylic resurfacer and color coats applied per manufacturer specs. 3-5 coats depending on condition. Weather-monitored scheduling.

Curing

24-72 hours of controlled curing. Courts stay closed during this window. We coordinate timing with your other trades so nobody's waiting on us.

5

Precision game lines per USTA, PPA, or custom spec. Multi-sport layouts color-coded for clarity. Final walkthrough and closeout docs delivered same day.

Frequently Asked Questions

Court Resurfacing Questions GCs Ask Us

What is the difference between court resurfacing and court refinishing?

Resurfacing involves removing the damaged top layer and applying a full new acrylic coating system — typically 3-5 coats including filler, color, and finish. Refinishing usually refers to a lighter refresh: cleaning, minor crack repair, and a new color coat. We assess the existing surface and recommend the right approach so you're not over-specifying or under-specifying in your bid.

How long does tennis court resurfacing take?

Most single-court resurfacing jobs take 5-7 business days from prep to final line striping. Multi-court projects at amenity centers typically run 7-12 days depending on court count and surface condition. We provide an exact timeline in every bid so you can schedule your other trades around it.

Do courts need to be closed during resurfacing?

Yes. Courts must be closed to foot traffic during the entire resurfacing process and for 24-72 hours after final coating for proper curing. For multi-court facilities, we can phase the work so some courts remain open while others are being resurfaced — we'll coordinate staging with your team.

How often do sport courts need to be resurfaced?

With a quality acrylic system like ATS Sports Acrytech, courts in Central Texas typically last 7-10 years before needing a full resurface. Cheap coatings often fail in 3-5 years — which means your client is paying for the job twice. We include the manufacturer's material warranty plus our 2-year workmanship warranty in every closeout package.

What surface system do you use for resurfacing?

We use ATS Sports Acrytech — a PPA-approved, professional-grade acrylic sport court system. It's engineered for high-traffic environments like multi-family amenity centers and stands up to Central Texas heat and UV exposure. The system includes acrylic resurfacer, color coating, and a textured playing surface.

Can you resurface a tennis court into pickleball courts?

Yes. Court conversion is a common scope for amenity center upgrades. We can overlay pickleball lines on an existing tennis court for dual-use, or re-stripe entirely for dedicated pickleball. We'll spec the right approach for your project and include it in the bid.

What do you need from us to bid a resurfacing project?

Court count, court type, approximate dimensions, and your project timeline. Photos of the existing surface help us nail the scope faster. If you have site plans, send them over — we'll turn around a detailed bid in 48 hours with line-item pricing you can drop straight into your estimate.
